Notable Players


Friedrich "Fritz" Walter was a German soccer player born on October 31, 1920 in Kaiserslautern, Germany. He started his career with 1. FC Kaiserslautern with their youth academy at nine years old, and made his first team debut at 17, playing as an attacking midfielder or forward.He played with the club until his retirement in 1959.

Walter made 364 appearances for 1. FC Kaiserslautern, from 1937 to 1959, and scored 357 goals with the club. He also captained the West German national team to their victory at the 1954 World Cup, and made 61 appearances and scored 33 goals with the national team.

Fritz Walter died on June 17, 2002 at the age of 81 in Enkenbach-Alsenborn, Germany, and is widely regarded to be one of the best players of his time. Fritz-Walter-Stadium is named after him.

During his first year of playing with them, Ballack was part of the team that won the Bundesliga that same year. He eventually became an important player for his next two years at the club, and made his first appearance for the German National Team in 1999. He was bought by fellow Bundesliga team Bayer Leverkusen for 4.1 million Euros in the same year. Ballack made 46 appearances for Kaiserslautern and scored four goals.

After Kaiserslautern, Ballack played for prestigious teams such as Bayern Munich in Germany and Chelsea FC in England. Ballack made over 200 appearances and scored 60 goals from 2002 to 2010. He retired in 2012.

After his retirement, Michael Ballack worked as a studio analyst for ESPN during the 2012 and 2016 European Championships and the 2014 World Cup.

Miroslav Josef Klose was born on June 9th, 1978 in Opole, Poland, where his father played for Polish team Odra Opole. Klose then moved to Kusel, Germany, only 40 kilometers away from Kaiserslautern. Klose joined 1. FC Kaiserslautern in 1999 from FC 08 Homburg, and made a total of 120 appearances and scoring 44 goals while with the team. In the 2001-02 season, he scored 16 goals, and was only two away from being the top scorer of the season.

Klose played for clubs such as Werder Bremen and Bayern Munich in Germany, and finished his career with Lazio in Italy.

Miroslav Klose's most notable moments were during his international appearances. He is Germany's top scorer of all time, the national team never lost a game in which Klose scored, and he won the World Cup with Germany in 2014, where he then ended his national team career. He offically retired in 2016.

He now coaches the Bayern Munich under-17 team.

Thomas Dooley was born on May 12, 1961 in Bechofen, West Germany to a German mother and an American father. He played as a defensive midfielder and was on the 1. FC Kaiserslautern Team from 1988 to 1993, where he made 128 appearances and scored 14 goals. Dooley also played for the United States National Team, where he made 81 appearances.

After being transferred, Dooley played for clubs such as Bayer Leverkusen and Shalke 04 in Germany and the Columbus Crew and the New York Metrostars in the United States.

Dooley has also worked as a manager, with his most recent job being with the Phillipines National Team, who he managed from 2014 to 2018.

Kevin Trapp was a goalkeeper for 1. FC Kaiserslautern from 2008 to 2012, where he spent most of his youth career at the club. After the club was relegated, Trapp transferred to newly promoted team Eintracht Frankfurt, finishing at 6th place that year.

Trapp then transferred to French team Paris Saint-Germain for a fee of 10 million Euros, and became their first team goalkeeper in 2015. With his time at the club, he won the Coupe de France, the Coupe de la Ligue, and the French Ligue 1 title.

Trapp currently plays at Eintracht Frankfurt, and has been with them since 2018.
